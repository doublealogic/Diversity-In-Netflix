How Has Netflix Changed?

The way we watch TV and movies continues to change. The pandemic changed it more dramatically with the closing of somme movie theaters permanently. We wanted to see how Netflix was changing their selection based on viewing habits. Do we prefer tv shows over movies? Has Netflix changed their selection to confirm this? Are we seeing a more diverse selection of media? Does Netflix include more shows and movies produced in other countries than the United States? These thoughts inspired us to dig a little deeper into the data. Since we all are users of Netflix, we wanted to see what changes they have made over the last three years.

  • Questions we asked:
  • Which country has the most representation on Netflix?
  • Which genres have the most representation? Which has the least?
  • Who is winning the war between platform dominance: movies or TV series?

Where we acquired data:

Kaggle dataset Netflix Movies and TV Shows. Trakt API
This dataset has movies and tv shows available on Netflix as of the end of 2019. The columns include: show id, Type, title, director, cast, country, date added to Netflix, release year, rating, and duration. We used postgres to host the database. Trakt api collects data on what TV shows and movies people are watching. We called data from the most watched movies database that shows us a watch count, play count, collected count, movie title, year, trakt id, IMDB id, and TMBD id. From the summary database, we called: country, genre, certification, homepage, and translations.
